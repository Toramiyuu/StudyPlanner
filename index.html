Exam Strategy & Tips

Paper 1
Learn definitions precisely ‚Äî examiners award marks for correct terminology like "ALU", "protocol", "compiler". Binary/hex questions are free marks with practice. Logic gates: draw truth tables for NAND, NOR, XOR ‚Äî they appear every session.
Paper 2 ‚Äî Scenario Question
Budget 30 minutes for the final 15-mark question. Write in proper pseudocode using the Cambridge notation (not Python). Show DECLARE statements, use proper indentation, and test your logic mentally before writing.
Trace Tables
Show every single step. One wrong variable value cascades errors. Use a ruler to keep rows aligned. Practice at least 10 past paper trace table questions before your exam.

Paper 1 ‚Äî Reading

‚è± 2 hours üìä 80 marks üìù 50% weighting

Questions based on two or three texts. Tests explicit and implicit understanding, inference, analysis of writer's craft, and the ability to select and use information. Also includes a shorter writing task (summary or directed writing).

Exam Strategy & Tips

Reading Paper
Quote precisely but briefly. Always explain the EFFECT of the writer's choice ‚Äî don't just identify techniques. Use the PEE structure: Point ‚Üí Evidence ‚Üí Explanation of effect. For summary questions, be concise and avoid lifting whole sentences.
Composition
Plan for 5 minutes before writing. Use a variety of sentence lengths. Open with something striking ‚Äî not "It was a dark and stormy night." End deliberately. Descriptive writing should engage ALL senses. Narrative should have a clear arc with tension.
SPaG Matters
Spelling, punctuation, and grammar are assessed in EVERY writing task. Use semicolons, colons, and dashes correctly for instant sophistication. Avoid comma splices ‚Äî they're the most common error.

Exam Strategy & Tips

Paper 4 is King (50%)
At 120 marks and 2 hours, Paper 4 is where your grade is largely decided. Practise structured questions under timed conditions. Show all working for calculations ‚Äî method marks are available even if the final answer is wrong.
Balance Your Revision
Don't neglect any of the three sciences. Physics (especially calculations) is often where students lose marks. Dedicate roughly equal time to Bio, Chem, and Physics. The MCQ paper covers all three equally.
Supplement Content
Extended paper questions often focus heavily on Supplement material (the starred content). Prioritise learning these topics ‚Äî they separate A*A*/AA from BB/CC.
Practical Skills
Whether Paper 5 or 6: know how to draw graphs correctly (labels, units, line of best fit), record measurements with correct significant figures, identify variables (independent, dependent, controlled), and evaluate experimental procedures. The planning question can come from any science.

Exam Strategy & Tips

Paper 1 (No Calculator)

Component 1 ‚Äî Written Exam

‚è± 1h 25min üìä 70 marks üìù 35% weighting

Four compulsory questions based on source documents about a global issue. Sources may include articles, data sets, personal testimonies, and images. You analyse, evaluate arguments, and make reasoned judgements.

Q1 (~14 marks): Identify/describe patterns from data. Q2 (~20 marks): Analyse arguments. Q3 (~16 marks): Evaluate quality of arguments. Q4 (~20 marks): Assess actions and explain judgements with evidence.

Component 2 ‚Äî Individual Report

üìä 60 marks üìù 30% weighting üìè 1500‚Äì2000 words

A research essay on a global question from the topic list. Must be your own work. Externally marked by Cambridge. Choose a topic you're genuinely interested in ‚Äî quality of research and personal reflection matter.

üìä 70 marks üìù 35% weighting ü§ù Collaborative

A team-based project: Explanation of Research & Planning (300‚Äì400 words, team), Evidence of Action (team), and a Reflective Paper (750‚Äì1000 words, individual). Internally assessed, externally moderated.

Exam Strategy & Tips

Written Exam
